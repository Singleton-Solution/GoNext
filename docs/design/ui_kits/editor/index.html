How we source single-origin beans.

Five years, eleven trips, and a few thousand cups later — here's how we decide what ends up in your cup.

Single-origin doesn't mean what most cafés say it means. It means traceable to a single farm, a single lot, a single harvest. Anything else is a region, which is fine, but it's a different promise.

What we actually look for.

Three things, in order: the farm's water access, the picker's wage, and the cup score. The first two are non-negotiable. The third is a starting point for conversation, not a final grade.
